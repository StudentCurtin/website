01. SQUASH
& STRETCH

01 - 12
NO ACTION HAPPENS
WITHOUT SQUASH &
STRETCH

Any organic subject made of flesh
will show movement within its form
during an action. The squash
position can depict the form either
flattened out by pressure or
bunched up and pushed together.
The stretch position shows the
same form in a very extended
condition. Think of how a sack half
full of flour behaves. Very little in an
object will move independently, the
face, for example, when the mouth
and jaw moves, the cheeks and
other areas are effected.

02. ANTICIPATION

02 - 12
THE AUDIENCE
SHOULD NEVER BE
CONFUSED OVER
HOW SOMETHING
HAPPENED

Anticipations leads the audience
from one idea to the next. It is the
storage and release of energy. The
audience should never be confused
over how something happened.
These actions might not explain
why, but there should be no doubt
about what. It is not always a
physical dynamic action. The
‘surprise gag’ is the opposite of
anticipation. It is when the audience
expects one thing and the animation
delivers another. An example of this
is often seen in over the top anime.
